Royal Convoy
Rules of Engagement

Two rival kingdoms. One perilous road. Only one convoy will reach the Royal Tourney in time.

I

The Road to the Tourney

Once a generation, the greatest kingdoms of the realm are summoned to the Royal Tourney — a grand tournament of arms, honour, and spectacle held at the neutral city of Valdenmoor. To be represented at the Tourney is a matter of immense prestige. To arrive without your Royals is a disgrace that echoes through the ages.

Two rival kingdoms have set out along the same ancient road through the wilderness — and they despise each other. Old grudges, disputed borders, and a century of bad blood have made this convoy as dangerous as any battlefield. Both sides know that if the other's Royals fail to arrive, their own claim to glory is uncontested.

And so the order has been given: reach the Royal Tourney at all costs — and make sure the enemy does not.

✦
II

The Quest

Royal Convoy is a two-player strategy game of escort, ambush, and fortune. Each player commands a convoy of ten units — soldiers, scouts, and specialists — charged with one sacred duty: guide your King and Queen safely across a dangerous kingdom to the Royal Tourney on the far side of the board.

The player who escorts both their Royals to the Royal Tourney first wins the game. Your non-Royal units can also make the crossing — earning them glory and a second chance at life — but the Royals are all that matter for victory.

✦

III

The Kingdom

The board is a 5-column, 16-row map of the kingdom — not every square is passable. Most of the terrain is shared open ground, but each player has a small number of private squares which the enemy cannot enter.

Movement flows downward (toward the tourney). Units cannot move upward, with the exception of the Thief unit.

Special Squares

Square Name Effect
🏰 Castle Units sheltering inside are protected from capture — except by the Siege.
🎲 Roll Again Landing here immediately grants you another roll and another move this turn.
💰 Gold Coin Landing here adds 1 coin to your treasury (2 for the Merchant).
🎪 Royal Tourney Your Royals must reach their own tourney square to win. Non-Royal units who arrive gain a life and return to the home castle.
✦
IV

Your Forces

Each player commands ten units. All begin at the home castle (off the board) and must be deployed by rolling dice and moving them onto the board from your starting square. Each unit has its own movement rules and special abilities.

Lives

Most non-Royal units carry two lives (shown as a Roman numeral badge: I, II, III). When captured they lose a life — reaching zero means permanent elimination (💀). The Soldier has unlimited lives and can never be permanently eliminated.

When a non-Royal unit reaches the Royal Tourney they gain one life (up to a maximum of three) and return to the home castle, ready to deploy again.

Kingsguard

The Knight, Shield, Siege, Archer, Sword, and Soldier are Kingsguard units. Any Royal standing orthogonally adjacent to a friendly Kingsguard unit is protected — the enemy cannot capture them while that guard is in place.

🤴 King ROY · KGSRD
−1 step. Moves one step fewer than the dice roll. Protected while adjacent to a Kingsguard. Must reach the Royal Tourney to win. Capturing a Royal seizes 1 coin from the enemy treasury.
👸 Queen ROY · KGSRD
−1 step. Same rules as the King. Both Royals must reach the Royal Tourney to claim victory. Capturing a Royal seizes 1 coin from the enemy treasury.
🐴 Knight KGSRD
+2 steps. Moves two steps more than the dice roll. Can reach the Royal Tourney without an exact count — generous arrival.
🛡️ Shield KGSRD
Absorbs first hit. The first melee strike against the Shield cracks it rather than capturing it. A cracked Shield can be captured normally on the next hit. Note: the Shield does not crack from an Archer attack — ranged arrows pass it by entirely.
⚙️ Siege KGSRD
Storms castles. The only unit that can capture an enemy sheltering inside a castle. Standard melee otherwise.
🗡️ Thief
Moves upward. Uniquely, the Thief can move toward your own end of the board. On capture, steals 1 coin from the enemy treasury.
🏹 Archer KGSRD
Range-1 kill. On landing, the Archer slays all enemies on orthogonally adjacent squares — without moving onto their square. Castle and guard protection apply. Must land on an empty square. Note: the Archer's arrows do not crack a Shield.
🤠 Merchant
Earns gold. +1 coin on a castle, +2 coins on a gold coin square. Can share a square with any one unit — friend or foe. Cannot spend coins to move.
⚔️ Sword KGSRD
Chain attack. Capturing with the Sword triggers a Chain — any subsequent kill by any unit that same turn continues the chain, granting another roll until a turn ends without a kill.
🚶 Soldier KGSRD
Unlimited lives. The Soldier cannot be permanently eliminated — no matter how many times captured, they return to the home castle. Standard movement.
✦
V

The Dice

Royal Convoy uses two custom dice, each showing the values 0, 1, 1, 2, 2, 3. On your turn you roll both and move one unit a number of steps equal to their combined total — between 0 and 6.

On the very first turn of the game (whoever goes first), only one die is rolled. This opening roll is a maximum of 3 steps.

⚔ Spending Gold
Instead of rolling, you may spend coins from your treasury to move a unit 1 step per coin. Royals and the Merchant cannot benefit from coin spending. This is your only way to guarantee a specific move — use it wisely.
✦
VI

Your Turn

Players alternate turns. On your turn, follow these steps:

1
Roll or Spend
Roll both dice (or spend gold coins instead). Your total is the number of steps you have to spend this move.
2
Choose a Unit
Select any of your units — on the board or still at the home castle. Units at the home castle deploy from your starting square, spending their first step to enter the road. Once you select a unit, all valid destination squares are highlighted in blue on the board.
3
Move
Move your chosen unit exactly the number of steps indicated (unless it's a Knight, who gets +2, or a Royal, who gets −1). You may not reverse direction mid-move.
4
Resolve Landing
Apply whatever effect the destination square has — capture an enemy, collect a coin, roll again, or shelter in a castle.
5
Pass the Turn
Your turn ends. If you rolled a 0 or had no valid moves, your turn passes automatically.
✦
VII

Combat

Most units capture by landing on an enemy's square. The captured unit is sent back to the home castle and loses one life. At zero lives, the unit is permanently eliminated.

Restrictions

You cannot capture a unit sheltering in a castle (unless you are the Siege). You cannot capture a Royal that is adjacent to an enemy Kingsguard unit — they are protected. You cannot land on a square occupied by your own units (unless you are the Merchant, who may share with anyone).

Capturing Royals

When a Royal is captured the attacker seizes 1 coin from the enemy treasury. However, Royals can only be captured when they have no adjacent Kingsguard. A well-escorted Royal is effectively untouchable.

The Shield's Defence

The first melee attack against a Shield unit cracks it rather than capturing it. The Shield stays on the board, now vulnerable. A second hit captures it normally. A Shield that returns to the home castle resets to full strength. Note that the Archer's ranged attack does not crack the Shield.

⚔ Chain Attack
When the Sword makes a capture, it triggers a Chain. While a Chain is active, any capture by any unit grants you another roll and another move. The chain continues until a turn passes with no kill.
✦
VIII

The Long Road Home

War takes its toll. When a unit is captured or completes the crossing to the Royal Tourney, it cannot immediately return to the road — it must first recover or make the long journey back to the home castle.

Any unit that is captured must rest and tend to its wounds. Any non-Royal unit that reaches the Royal Tourney must journey all the way back before it can ride out again. In both cases, the unit is placed in the home castle and must wait four of your own turns before it may re-enter the road.

While a unit is resting, a countdown number is shown over its portrait in the home castle — ticking down from 4 to 1 each turn. When the counter disappears, the unit is ready to march again.

⚜ Note on the Soldier
The Soldier's boundless courage means they can never be permanently eliminated — but even the Soldier needs four turns to recover from capture before returning to the fray.

IX

The Four Kingdoms

Before the convoy departs, each commander must choose which kingdom they represent. Four great kingdoms have answered the call to Valdenmoor — each led by a king of distinct character, accompanied by his queen, and blessed with a unique advantage on the road.

🤴 King John the Benevolent
Queen: Andrea
Bonus: Treasury Head Start. King John begins the journey with 3 gold coins already in his treasury — enough to guarantee a decisive early move when the dice are unkind.
🤴 King Adam the Brave
Queen: Anastassiya
Bonus: Hardened Warriors. King Adam's Sword and Archer begin the campaign with an extra life — starting at III lives rather than II. They are harder to eliminate and can absorb punishment that would fell a lesser warrior.
🤴 King Joshua the Wise
Queen: Amy
Bonus: Swift Recovery. King Joshua's kingdom is known for its healers and swift logistics. All of his units rest for only 2 turns at the home castle after capture or crossing — half the time of other kingdoms.
🤴 King Elijah the Brutal
Queen: Kerylyn
Bonus: The Relentless Knight. King Elijah's Knight is a creature of unstoppable momentum. When activated, the Knight may choose to move the rolled amount, plus 1, or plus 2 — all valid destination squares are highlighted simultaneously, and the rider picks whichever suits the moment.
⚜ Choosing Your Kingdom
After selecting a game mode, each player chooses their kingdom in turn. P1 selects first, then P2 or the CPU (who picks randomly). No two commanders may lead the same kingdom. In Hard Mode, P1's eliminatable units begin with only 1 life — choose your kingdom bonus wisely.
✦
X

Victory

The game ends the moment a player escorts both their King and Queen to the Royal Tourney. That player wins — their Royals arrive in glory, and the rival kingdom's claim to the tourney is dust.

Non-Royal units that reach the Royal Tourney return to the home castle with a bonus life (maximum three) after their four-turn rest. Sending non-Royal units ahead is never wasted — it builds resilience for the escorts who guard your Royals.

There is no draw condition. Convoys reach the tourney, or they fall.

⚜ Counsel for New Commanders
  • Protect your Royals above all else — keep Kingsguard adjacent to them at all times.
  • Guard your Siege carefully; it is the only unit that can storm a castle, and once lost, any enemy who shelters inside is beyond your reach.
  • Consider holding your Royals at the home castle in the early going — clearing enemy units from the road first can make the Royal crossing far safer.
  • Use the Merchant to build your treasury, then spend coins at decisive moments.
  • Castle squares are your best shelter when under threat — seek them out with your key units.
  • Never underestimate the Soldier — they cannot be removed from the field permanently.
